19

Sales Force Method

 

 

  • Statistical Methods
  •  Trend Method- It provides a rough trend of the forecast on the basis of past experience
  •  Graphical Method- Sales data are plotted on graph paper and a graph is drawn for a number of years
  •  Time Series Methods- It is used for long periods duly taking into account cyclical changes, seasonal variations and irregular fluctuations.
  •  The time series method shows the future trends of sales. The various techniques that can be used for determining these trends are:
  •   Freehold or Graphical Method- A trend line is fitted by freehand to know future sales
  •   Semi-Average Method- Data are divided into two parts, preferably with the same number of years
  •   Moving Average Method- A trend is determined by moving averages
  •   Method of Least Squares- The straight line is represented by the equation: yc=a+bx

Territory Management

 

 

  • A sales territory comprises of a group of customers or a geographical area assigned to a sales unit.
  • A sales territory represents a group of customer accounts, an industry, a market or a specific geographical area.
  • Territory management includes the market potential, number of customer accounts, the firms experience and market share in the territory, the capability of the salesperson assigned and the frequency of sales calls made.

Why establish sales territories

 

 

  • A company develop sales territories for various reasons
  • To obtain entire coverage of the market
  • To establish a salesperson’s responsibility
  • To evaluate performance
  • To improve customer relations
  • To reduce sales expenses
  • To allow better matching of salesperson to customer
  • To benefit salespeople and the company

Sales Analysis 

 

  • Sales analysis is the detailed examination of sales volume by territory, salesperson, customer, product line etc.
  • Sales analysis by territory- In this method, the sales force is controlled on the basis of territory. It assumes that each quota assigned to each salesperson is based on fair and sound measurement i.e. potential.
  • Sales Analysis by Salesperson- It studies the sales performance of all salespersons working in the territory.

 

29

  • Sales Analysis by Product Line- It sees the performance on the basis of product line where the performance is poor.
  • Sales Analysis by Customer- Customer-wise break up of sales helps to understand the target market and where the sales manager has to put more emphasis.
